The Discovery

Following on from a discovery into viability with Southwark Council, the Connected Places Catapult convened a partnership with Southwark, Tower Hamlets, the Greater London Authority and FutureGov to take the work - and the resulting concepts into an alpha phase.

  • Read about the discovery

The Problem

The crux of the problem to be solved is that developers - armed with data and the promise of more homes in the midst of a housing crisis - are able to use the viability process to negotiate lower levels of affordable housing and councils lack the information, tools and confidence to push back against them.

The Alpha

Through alpha we tested the concepts from discovery - namely the ‘viability assessment checker’ and the ‘review and comparison tool’ - with users to ensure they were feasible, viable and desirable before proceeding to develop prototypes.

Our findings from this testing allowed us to refine our focus in the alpha and update our problem statement from ‘How might we prototype a better way to evaluate viability assessments?’ to ‘How might we unlock the value of data in viability assessments?’

Outputs

You can read the story of the project, what we did, what we found and our suggested next steps in the final narrative deck.

With the importance of data as a key enabler in this space, we developed a viability data schema in concert with other projects and organisations dedicated to planning. We prototyped the comparison tool as one example of a digital intervention that could be powered by viability data, supporting council officers to have better, more informed negotiations about what is viable where. you can try this out by clicking the link below or watch the video.
